Overview

I refined the page flow and interaction design of the “Repayment Query” pages to improve the efficiency of repayment tracking and post-loan services, ultimately increasing company revenue.

Problems

The product team noticed that traffic to the “Repayment Query” page is low, and CTA click rates for both financial products and repayments remain unsatisfactory.

Pain Points & Solutions

To understand users' motivation and ability to use “Repayment Query” and identify issues in its presentation, I collaborated with the customer service team. We conducted phone surveys, asked users about their experience and recorded their feedback.

Business Goals

While solving user pain points, I helped the product meet business goals—boosting financial product usage and reducing overdue repayments.

How might we improve the loan repayment experience while encouraging greater financial product usage and reducing overdue repayments?

Project Launch Results

The redesign streamlined navigation, significantly increasing engagement. Key CTA buttons experienced a surge in clicks, while page views and user interactions improved remarkably.

Design Process

I identified pain points, analysed user scenarios, optimised the information hierarchy, and conducted usability testing. Lastly, I supported development and testing, tracking post-launch data.

User Journey

By creating a user journey, I pinpointed areas where clearer communication, better incentives, or smoother payment flows could improve user experience.

Scenario Analysis

I analysed what information users needed most and actions users could take in different status scenarios and adjusted the information hierarchy to reduce confusion.

Usability Testing

I created specific tasks for usability testing to avoid unsupported actions, while ensuring that financial products remained visible and users understood they could activate them once their status changed.

Future Considerations

Jumping straight into a complete redesign of the repayment query feature carries risks. In the future, we could conduct A/B tests by initially releasing the new design to a small group of users, gathering feedback, and then launching the effective version to everyone.
