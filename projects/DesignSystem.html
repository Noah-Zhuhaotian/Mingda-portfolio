Unifying Design for Cross-Border Payments

UX/UI Design

Overview

I organised the design system for Kuaiqian's international cross-border payment platform to unify designs across merchant and customer platforms, improving consistency and development efficiency.

Problems

As the UX designer for cross-border products, I noticed that the inconsistent design styles between the merchant platform and operation platform made it unclear which standard to follow, causing difficulties for designers and lowering efficiency.

Solutions

I designed a well-organised design system to ensure a consistent cross-platform user experience while streamlining the design process and improving team collaboration.

Project Goals

How might we build a clear and accessible design system that ensures consistent use of components, and simplifies decision-making for the team?

User Satisfaction

User testing showed that the design system was well-received by the cross-border team, enhancing collaboration between designers, developers, and product managers.

Design Process

Outside of my daily tasks, I led the research, organised and design the design system with my fellow designer, and collected user feedback for ongoing improvements.

Challenges

We had been using Ant Design components but selectively chose those that matched our platform. We needed a more customised design system rather than the complexity of Ant Design.

Information Architecture

I reviewed the current platform designs, categorised the design elements, and built the structure for our design system, defining the foundations, key components, and scope.

Design Components Scope

I identified the most commonly used design components from the platform's features and workflows, then organised them into categories.

Key Learning

Reviewing and analysing the platform's design helped me deepen my understanding of how each design element is used. Organising the design system gave me a broader perspective on the complexities involved, allowing me to think more systematically about better layouts and interactions for an improved user experience.

Future Considerations

The design system focused solely on unifying design elements, but the front-end team also had its own component library. Bridging the two could significantly boost development efficiency.
