Enhancing Internal Comms for the MOJ

UX/UI Design

Overview

Our MUXD project team designed an internal platform for the MOJ Comms Team to showcase its services and updates, provide easy access to resources, and streamline document management.

Timeline

9 weeks

The Team

4 UX major students

My Role

UX designer:

Research, Testing

IA Design (Project Folder)

Homepage Refinement

Prototyping

Problems

The client's document management was disorganised, making collaboration difficult for internal staff. External users also struggled to find the team's services on the homepage, affecting service efficiency.

Solutions

We designed the IA and folder structure for easier file access, improved project document management for better tracking, and optimised the homepage to promote team services and boost employee engagement.

Project Goals

How might we design a SharePoint repository that streamlines content management, promotes Comms services, and enhances homepage usability for better cross-team collaboration?

Client Satisfaction

The client was content with the IA structure and the information displayed on the homepage and planned to gradually improve their current SharePoint site based on the prototype we provided.

Process

We identified user needs through several rounds of client interviews, structured document categories with card sorting, and tested IA usability with tree testing. We then designed the homepage and refined it based on usability testing feedback.

User Groups

We interviewed 11 employees from different roles within the client team, gathering insights to create user personas and identify key pain points.

Design Workshop

To organise the cluttered files in the client's SharePoint repository and align the information architecture with user habits, we held a card-sorting workshop and tree-testing to gather user feedback.

Information Architecture

By reorganising the original repository, we structured the content into four sections and redesigned the IA. I shifted the project folder structure from team-focused to project-focused to improve collaboration.

User Flow

To improve project management efficiency and reduce maintenance effort, I mapped out internal project management workflows and then designed features to enhance their experience at each stage.

Challenges

The client requested a solution for external users to submit service requests, but this was beyond our original design scope. We also lacked sufficient research on the request approval process.

Key Pages

Show team leaders and managers in the sidebar for easy connection across units.

Collapse the services section for seamless browsing of other key content.

Quickly navigate to the document repository page via the top tab menu.

Easily track and manage projects with automated data sources for simple, sustainable maintenance.

Future Considerations

Scope creep caused time constraints in this project, and without developer support, we couldn't optimise the design to align with the development team's capabilities. I need to improve my ability to manage client needs and communicate design strategies more effectively.
